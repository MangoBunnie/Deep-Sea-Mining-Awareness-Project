Deep Sea Mining is a Hidden Threat

Deep sea mining threatens fragile marine ecosystems through pollution, habitat destruction, and long-term environmental damage.

Take Action

What is Deep Sea Mining?

Deep sea mining involves extracting mineral deposits from the ocean floor, typically deeper than 200 meters, using large remote-controlled machinery.

Why it exists

  • Materials for batteries
  • Smartphones and electronics
  • Aerospace and infrastructure

Key resources extracted

  • Polymetallic nodules (manganese, nickel, cobalt)
  • Cobalt-rich crusts
  • Polymetallic sulfides (copper, gold, zinc)

Environmental Impact

  • Destruction of deep-sea ecosystems
  • Marine life takes extremely long to recover
  • Sediment plumes spread far beyond mining sites
  • Noise and light pollution disrupt marine species
  • Water contamination affects ocean health

These impacts can extend across entire ecosystems, not just the mining area.

Better Alternatives

Recycling

Reusing batteries and electronics reduces demand for new mining.

Innovation

Develop longer-lasting and more sustainable technology.

Reuse Materials

Recover minerals from waste and industrial byproducts.
